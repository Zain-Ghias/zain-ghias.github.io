About Me

Hi, I’m Zain, a third-year Applied Statistics student at the University of Toronto Mississauga, with minors in Computer Science and Mathematics.

I’m passionate about connecting data, design, and storytelling by using analysis and visualization to make information more human and useful.

Current Work

I’m currently a Software Engineer Intern at Wholeviz, a data visualization platform helping NGOs, journalists, and public teams turn datasets into interactive dashboards. My work focuses on React, Next.js, Tailwind, and Plotly, where I design intuitive front-end interfaces and experiment with AI-assisted data visualization features.

Recently, I was selected for the Skills for the Future Program with IBM SkillsBuild × Riipen, collaborating with Digital Placemaking Canada on website redesign and data-driven dashboards.

My startup experience has taught me to be adaptable, communicate effectively across different teams, and deliver results in fast-paced environments.

More About Me

Alongside my work, I’m completing the IBM Data Analytics Professional Certificate on Coursera and the IBM SkillsBuild Digital Credential in User Experience Design Fundamentals. Through these programs, I’m deepening my skills in Excel, SQL, Python (Pandas), and IBM Cognos Analytics, while learning to bridge analytical storytelling with UI and UX design thinking.

Outside of code and coursework, I recharge through volleyball, table tennis, and long walks around Mississauga. I’m naturally curious about how things work, whether that’s a dataset, a business model, or a React component, and I like turning that curiosity into projects that make data actually mean something.
